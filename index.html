📖 How to use Planner

1. The Navigation & Layout

  • Scroll horizontally inside the planner to view more weeks. Holding Shift while scrolling with a mouse wheel will scroll sideways.
  • Pan by dragging: Click and drag empty space inside the planner area to pan left/right and up/down quickly.
  • Toggle Zoom (Weeks / Days / Months): Click the 🔍 Zoom: Weeks button in the top toolbar to cycle between three zoom levels — full Weeks (default), granular Days (Mon–Fri columns), and wide Months overview. Note: 5 days = 1.0 week in duration.
  • Adjust visible year range: Use the small + / − year controls in the toolbar to add years before or after the current timeline, or remove the last year.
  • Adjust Columns: Hover your mouse over the vertical border line separating the left-side text columns from the grid. You can drag these borders left or right from *anywhere* vertically along the column!
  • Comments column: Click the Comments button to hide or show the left comments column and free up more timeline space.
  • Hide Buttons: Click Hide Buttons to collapse the toolbar rows and leave more room for the planner itself. Click it again to bring the controls back.
  • Highlight a Column: Click any week/day/month header in the timeline to highlight that whole column. Click the same header again to remove the highlight. The highlighted column is saved with your CSV.
  • Today Line: Click the 📌 Today button to jump the planner to the real current date and reset the red line to system today. Right-click that same button to show or hide the red line. You can drag the top TODAY handle, or drag near the red line in empty timeline space, to reposition it temporarily. Double-click the top handle to reset it back to today.
  • Highlight a Row: Click empty space on a row in the timeline area to highlight that full row. Click it again to remove the row highlight.
  • Custom Columns: Click + Add Column to create extra sticky columns for values such as risk, owner, phase, or status. Their data is saved in CSV, and each custom column can also be filtered.

2. Structure & Hierarchy

  • Families -> Projects -> Activities & Milestones: This planner supports deep nesting.
  • Add Family: Click the 👥 Add Family button in the top toolbar. It instantly creates a full Parent Family group populated with predefined Brother/Child Projects.
  • Add top-level rows: Use Add Project, Add Milestone, or Add Activity to create new top-level items at the end of the current plan.
  • Add Sub-Items: Look at the gray buttons next to a row's name. Use +P (Add Project), +M (Add Milestone), or +A (Add Activity) to nest items *inside* that specific row.
  • Templates: Click Add Template to create reusable activity templates (name, duration, color) and optional master templates made from several activity steps in sequence.
  • Collapse/Expand: Click the small triangle (▼ / ▶) next to a parent item to hide or show its children. *Note: When collapsed, any milestones within that parent are automatically condensed and shown on the parent's row!*

3. Modifying Items

  • Rename: Click on any text in the blue/gray column to edit it directly. Projects, Milestones, and newly created Activities open smart dropdowns for selecting predefined templates.
  • Assign Team: Click Add Team in the top toolbar to add people to your roster. Back in the grid, click the user icon (👤) under any row's settings gear to assign people. Their names will appear directly on the colored timeline bars. You can check workload clashes in the Team modal.
  • Reorder & Parent: Grab the vertical dot handles (⋮⋮) on the far left of a row to drag and drop it above or below other items.
    • Nest: Hold Alt while dropping on a row to nest the item inside it.
    • Un-nest: Hold Ctrl (or Cmd) while dropping to make the item a top-level parent.
    • Move to End: Scroll to the very bottom and drop on the placeholder row to move top-level.
  • Multi-select Rows: Use the checkboxes on the far left to select one or more rows. Hold Shift while clicking another checkbox to select a visible range. When anything is selected, the bottom action bar lets you move, recolor, assign, lock, unlock, or delete the selected rows. Press Esc to clear the selection.
  • Change Colors: Click the gear icon (⚙) in the header to reveal the row controls, then click the color circle to paint the bar or diamond.
  • Edit dates directly: You can also type dates or week values directly in row fields such as milestone week boxes for more precise scheduling.
  • Duplicate: Click the green D button to instantly clone a row. *Warning: Duplicating a parent will also recursively clone ALL of its children!*
  • Delete: Click the red × button. *Deleting a parent deletes everything inside it!*

4. Timeline Constraints & Locking

  • Move Activities: Drag the center of a colored bar to change its start date.
  • Resize Activities: Drag the darker right-edge handle of an activity block to stretch its duration.
  • Completion % (Activities): Open the row settings with the gear icon (⚙), then type a number (0–100) in the % field next to an Activity row. A semi-transparent progress fill appears on the left side of the timeline bar, showing how much of the work is done. This value is saved in CSV.
  • Move Milestones: Drag the diamond block itself. Alternatively, use the YYWW text input box next to a milestone's name to precisely jump it to a specific Year/Week (e.g. `2648`).
  • Row Sharing (Milestones): Use the small ▲ / ▼ buttons next to a Milestone to change its "Row Layer". Assigning two milestones to the same layer makes them render side-by-side on the same horizontal track!
  • The Lock Button (🔒): Clicking the Lock button will lock that item's time constraints. Crucially, locking a row automatically locks everything visually underneath it. Dragging that locked anchor will physically push/pull *every* locked item below it forward or backward in time!
  • Global Lock: Use the toolbar Lock: ON/OFF button to lock or unlock all items quickly.
  • Isolating a Lock: Hold the CTRL key while clicking a lock button to explicitly lock or unlock ONLY that item, independently of its children. Dragging a locked parent will *skip* and leave behind any manually unlocked descendants!

5. Filtering Data

  • Filter by Name: Click the ▼ icon next to the "Project" column header. Uncheck names to dynamically hide matching rows.
  • Filter by Type: Click the clipboard (📋) icon to filter strictly by Families, Projects, Tasks, or Milestones.
  • Filter by Custom Column: If you added your own columns, use the small filter control in that column header to hide rows that match selected values.

6. Add comments

  • Add/Toggle Comment: Hold CTRL and Click on any colored bar or milestone diamond in the grid to open a sticky comment popup.
  • Orange Indicators: Any block with a comment will show a small orange (amber) dot in its corner, letting you know at a glance where notes are located.
  • The Connector: A dynamic connecting line (tail) always points from the comment box to the center of its block, no matter how far you drag the popup.
  • Interact: You can drag the gray header area of a comment to move it, and use the bottom-right handle to resize it.
  • Delete: Click the trash icon (🗑️) in the comment header to permanently delete the comment.
  • Persistence: Block comments are automatically saved and restored when you export/import CSV files.

7. Dependency Links

  • Enter Link Mode: Click the 🔗 Link button in the toolbar. Blue anchor dots appear on every bar and milestone diamond — one on each end.
  • Draw a Link: In Link Mode, click the source anchor dot (start or end of a bar), then click the target anchor dot on another bar. A dashed bezier wire is drawn between them.
  • Exit Link Mode: Click the 🔗 Link button again to turn off the mode. Existing wires remain visible.
  • Delete a Link: Hover over a wire to reveal the red ✕ button at its midpoint, then click it.
  • Toggle Wire Visibility: Click 👁 Links: On/Off in the toolbar to show or hide all dependency wires without deleting them. The visibility state is saved in your CSV.
  • Auto-propagation: When you drag an item that has outgoing links, all linked items are automatically shifted by the same number of weeks, keeping the dependency chain intact.

8. Advanced Utilities & Alarms

  • Holidays: Click the Add Holiday button in the toolbar to open the Holiday Manager. Add individual dates (YYYY-MM-DD) or date ranges. Holiday columns are shaded gray in the grid, and task durations automatically skip over them so working-day counts stay accurate. Holidays are saved in your CSV.
  • 🔔 Alarms: Click Add Alarm to open the sidebar. You can create reminders manually there, double-click a week/day header to create a calendar alarm for that date, or double-click a row timeline cell to create an alarm tied to that item. Click 📅 Export to Outlook (.ics) to download them as calendar invites.
  • Shared Master CSV / Auto-save: Use Open File to choose a shared master CSV for this browser. After that, the planner can auto-save your edits back to the same file and periodically re-read it for external updates. If write permission is not available, the file opens read-only. This is a shared last-write-wins workflow, not an exclusive edit lock.
  • Export JPEG: Captures the current visible planner view as a JPEG, using the on-screen layout and hiding the toolbar button rows in the exported image.
  • Save to CSV: Backs up your entire planner into a standard spreadsheet file. This perfectly preserves your hierarchy, colors, column widths, active filters, custom alarms, dependency links, team assignments, completion percentages, holidays, custom columns, templates, and the current zoom level.
  • Load CSV: Overwrites your current session with a saved file.
  • Merge CSV: Loads a selected CSV and carefully appends its rows into your current planner without overwriting the existing one. It also merges supported linked data such as alarms, links, templates, template folders, people, holidays, and custom columns where possible.

9. Activity & Master Templates

  • Create Standard Templates: Click Add Template to open the modal. Here you can define common activities by Name, Duration (Days), and Color. You can even use decimals like `0.5` days to represent 4-hour slots!
  • Folders & Parameterized Templates: In the same modal you can group templates into folders/categories, and you can mark Name, Duration, and/or Color as Ask on apply so the user customizes those fields each time the template is used.
  • Create Master Templates: The Template Modal allows you to drag-and-drop your basic templates into the right-side Composer. This lets you string together sequential steps (e.g., *Design -> Review -> Build*). When you save this Master Template, deploying it onto your planner automatically generates the parent row and all of its sequential sub-activities in one click!
  • Edit & Fork Masters: Click any Master Template in your saved list to load its setup back into the Composer. You can adjust the quantities, add new steps, or change colors. Click Update Master to overwrite it, or click Save as New to fork it into a brand new standalone Master Template!
